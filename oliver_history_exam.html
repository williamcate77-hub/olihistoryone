Question 11
Define a primary source.
Expected AnswerA primary source is created during the time period being studied. It contains first-hand information about history.
Question 12
Define a secondary source.
Expected AnswerA secondary source is created after the time period. It interprets or explains the past.
Question 13
Give three examples of primary sources.
Expected AnswerAny three from: diary entry, photograph, coin, artefact, eyewitness account, spear points.
Question 14
Give three examples of secondary sources.
Expected AnswerAny three from: school history textbook, documentary, movie about Tutankhamun, newspaper article written later, statue, history book.
Question 15
Explain why historians must compare sources.
Expected AnswerHistorians compare sources to avoid bias and mistakes. No single source tells the whole story — by comparing multiple sources, historians can build a more accurate and complete picture of the past.
Question 16
Explain why bias can affect historical sources.
Expected AnswerBias means a one-sided view. Sources can be biased because the person who created them had their own perspective, opinions, or reasons for recording information a certain way. This means the source may not show the full truth.
Question 17
Describe how farming changed human society.
Expected AnswerFarming meant people could grow their own food and stay in one place. This led to larger, settled communities and caused rapid population growth because there was more food available to support more people.

Question 41
Explain why timelines are important to historians.
Expected AnswerTimelines help historians organise events in chronological order. They allow us to see when events happened, how much time passed between them, and identify patterns of continuity and change across long periods of history.
Question 42
Describe what CE and BCE mean.
Expected AnswerCE means Common Era — the period after year 1, equivalent to AD. BCE means Before Common Era — the period before year 1, equivalent to BC. These terms are used as an alternative to AD and BC.
Question 43
Describe two injuries found on Narrabeen Man.
Expected AnswerNarrabeen Man had damage to his skull. Spear fragments were also found embedded in his spine. His head was discovered approximately 40 cm from the rest of his body, suggesting violent separation.
Question 44
Explain what the spear fragments found in Narrabeen Man suggest.
Expected AnswerThe spear fragments suggest that Narrabeen Man was attacked and killed with spears. This is physical evidence that supports the interpretation that he was involved in violent conflict, possibly with members of another clan.
Question 45
Explain why historians are unsure about how Narrabeen Man died.
Expected AnswerHistorians are unsure because Narrabeen Man died approximately 4,000 years ago. There are no written records and no eyewitnesses. The only evidence is physical — the remains and injuries — which must be interpreted. Interpretations can be wrong.
Question 46
Describe what serendipity means and give an example.
Expected AnswerSerendipity means making an accidental discovery. The word was invented because so many important historical finds have been made by accident. An example is the discovery of Narrabeen Man — found by contractors digging for pipes and sewers in 2005, not by historians searching for remains.
Question 47
Explain the difference between a fact and an interpretation.
Expected AnswerA fact is something that can be proven as definitely true. An interpretation is an explanation based on evidence that cannot be absolutely proven. For example, it is a fact that Narrabeen Man had spear fragments in his spine. It is an interpretation that he was killed by another clan.

Question 71
Explain why accidental discoveries are important in history.
Expected AnswerAccidental discoveries (serendipity) are important because many of history's most significant finds were not planned. Without them, key evidence about the past would never have been uncovered. Narrabeen Man is a prime example — his remains were found by chance during construction work.
Question 72
Describe what Mesopotamian cuneiform is.
Expected AnswerCuneiform is one of the earliest systems of writing, developed in Mesopotamia around 3,000 BCE. It used wedge-shaped symbols pressed into clay tablets to record information such as trade, laws, and events.
Question 73
Explain why physical evidence is important when written records do not exist.
Expected AnswerWhen no written records exist, physical evidence is the only way historians can learn about the past. Objects, remains, and artefacts provide direct clues about how people lived and died. Without the physical evidence from Narrabeen Man's remains, historians would know nothing about his life or death.
Question 74
Describe how population growth changed human life.
Expected AnswerPopulation growth led to larger communities, which required better organisation, food production, and eventually the development of cities and civilisations. As populations grew, people needed to develop new systems of trade, writing, and governance to manage larger groups.
Question 75
Explain how distance and migration are connected in early human history.
Expected AnswerEarly humans had to travel enormous distances to populate different parts of the world. The distance from Africa to Australia is approximately 12,595 km — a journey that took thousands of years. Migration spread humans across the globe, leading to the diverse civilisations we see in history.
Question 76
Describe the key features of the Narrabeen Man discovery.
Expected AnswerNarrabeen Man was discovered in January 2005 near the Ocean Street bus stop at Narrabeen, when contractors dug up his remains while installing pipes. The bones are approximately 4,000 years old. Evidence of injuries included skull damage, spear fragments in the spine, and the head found 40 cm from the body. The remains are currently housed at the University of Sydney, with ongoing discussions about their final resting place.
Question 77
Explain why six different ways of measuring time are used in history.
Expected AnswerHistory covers enormous spans of time, so different measures are needed to describe different scales. Seconds and minutes measure short durations, while days, weeks, months, and years measure longer ones. For ancient history, we use centuries, millennia, and systems like BCE and CE to describe events thousands of years ago without needing to count every single year.
